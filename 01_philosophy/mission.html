Tengri Language
Open Source

Mission & Philosophy

Tengri is a modern, high-performance programming language inspired by the agglutinative morphology of the Kazakh language. Our mission is to turn linguistic clarity into computational clarity: a language that is expressive, safe, and fast from AST → VM → JIT → AOT — built for research, systems, and AI-driven workloads.

Why Tengri — and why agglutinative roots?

Many languages evolved around English-like syntax. We take a different route: agglutinative forms enable compact yet explicit composition of meaning. Tengri explores how this linguistic structure can streamline parsing and lower intermediate representations, improving compiler simplicity and opening new optimization opportunities.

  • Clarity by construction: morphology-inspired scoping and namespacing reduce ambiguity.
  • Low ceremony: first-class expressions, lightweight blocks, predictable evaluation.
  • Performance path: a single semantics across AST → VM → JIT → AOT enables incremental speedups without rewriting code.

Design Principles

  • Expressive minimalism: small core, powerful standard patterns.
  • Determinism: what you write is what runs; visible costs.
  • Safety first: explicit mutation, clear lifetimes, simple error semantics.
  • International by default: Unicode-ready, inclusive tooling, i18n docs.

Performance Roadmap

  1. AST Interpreter: correctness, developer UX, REPL.
  2. Register VM: lean bytecode, peephole passes, constant folding.
  3. Tiered JIT: hot-path fusion, inlining, bounds elimination.
  4. AOT: SSA-based optimizer, profile-guided codegen, link-time specialization.

What success looks like

  • Productivity: read/write ergonomics rivaling Python/JS; tooling that feels instant.
  • Performance: VM > Python by multiples; JIT approaching Go/Rust on numeric kernels.
  • Interoperability: seamless FFI with C/Go/Rust; easy embedding and sandboxing.
  • Reliability: deterministic builds, reproducible benchmarks, verified semantics.

Open Community

  • MIT License: permissive and business-friendly.
  • Transparent benchmarks: CSV + plots checked into the repo; reproducible scripts.
  • Contributor first: clear onboarding, good first issues, mentoring.

Governance & Ethics

  • Meritocratic + inclusive: technical proposals evaluated openly.
  • Respect for language & culture: celebrating Kazakh roots while serving a global audience.
  • Sustainability: funding-neutral roadmap; transparent sponsorship.
